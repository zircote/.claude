# HMH Messaging by Audience

Tailored messaging guidance for different HMH audiences.

## Teachers

### What They Care About
- Time savings
- Student engagement
- Ease of use
- Data to inform instruction
- Professional growth
- Support when needed

### Key Messages
- "Spend more time teaching, less time planning"
- "Tools designed with your busy life in mind"
- "Real-time insights to reach every learner"
- "Professional support when you need it"

### Tone
- Empathetic, supportive
- Practical, solution-focused
- Respectful of their expertise
- Celebratory of their dedication

### Proof Points
- Teacher testimonials
- Time saved statistics
- Student growth data
- Professional learning options

### Don't
- Imply technology replaces them
- Oversimplify their challenges
- Use condescending language
- Assume uniform tech comfort

## Administrators/District Leaders

### What They Care About
- Student outcomes and growth
- ROI and budget justification
- Implementation support
- Data and reporting
- Equity and access
- State standards alignment

### Key Messages
- "Accelerate student growth across your district"
- "One integrated platform, fewer complications"
- "Data-driven insights for informed decisions"
- "Partnership support from planning to implementation"

### Tone
- Strategic, results-focused
- Professional, credible
- Partnership-oriented
- Evidence-based

### Proof Points
- District success stories
- Research efficacy data
- ROI calculations
- Implementation support details
- ESSA evidence ratings

### Don't
- Focus only on features
- Ignore budget realities
- Overpromise outcomes
- Dismiss existing programs

## Parents/Families

### What They Care About
- Their child's success
- Understanding what's being taught
- How to help at home
- Progress visibility
- Safety and privacy

### Key Messages
- "Supporting your child's learning journey"
- "Stay connected to classroom progress"
- "Resources to extend learning at home"
- "Committed to student privacy and safety"

### Tone
- Warm, approachable
- Clear, jargon-free
- Supportive, encouraging
- Trustworthy

### Don't
- Use education jargon
- Assume familiarity with programs
- Overlook accessibility needs
- Ignore privacy concerns

## Channel Considerations

### Email
- Personalize by role
- Lead with their priority benefit
- One clear CTA
- Mobile-optimized

### Phone/Sales
- Listen first
- Address specific challenges
- Offer relevant proof points
- Propose clear next step

### Events/Conferences
- Interactive demonstrations
- Educator-to-educator testimonials
- Hands-on product experience
- Follow-up materials

### Digital Advertising
- Role-targeted messaging
- Benefit-focused headlines
- Strong visuals
- Clear landing page alignment
